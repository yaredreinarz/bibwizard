## How to read the results

- **Top hit matches expected paper, high confidence (≥ 0.8)** — works as designed.
- **Top hit matches expected paper, low confidence (0.5–0.7)** — works, but the entailment check is being cautious; this is fine for hard paraphrases.
- **Top hit is the WRONG paper** — interesting. Usually means the paraphrase happens to align better with another paper's vocabulary than the source's. Try paraphrasing closer to the ground-truth wording and see if it flips.
- **Empty result on a positive case (1–10)** — the entailment check is too strict, or the chunk happened to land outside the top-20 candidate pool. Re-run with `--pool 40` to widen.
- **High-confidence hit on a negative control (N1, N2)** — false positive. Note the claim and the quoted passage so the entailment prompt can be tightened.

## Quick commands

```powershell
# Single claim:
bibwizard cite "Single-mode fibers reduce the modal noise that plagues multi-mode fiber feeds..."

# Widen the search if the default 20-chunk pool is missing the right passage:
bibwizard cite "..." --pool 40

# In chat:
bibwizard chat
> find a citation that justifies the following: <claim>
```
